1867

John Stuart MP suggested giving women the vote.

1897

The NUWSS was founded (see suffragists).

1900

Bill on votes for women refused.

1903

WSPU founded (see suffragettes).

1903

Christabel Pankhurst tried to bring the suffragists and suffragettes together but Millicent Fawcett refused.

1908

The suffragette movement intensified: Edith New chained herself to Downing Street gates to make speeches so the police couldn’t move her.

1909

The PM Churchill was attacked in a GWR station in Bristol because he refused to give the vote to women.

1911

PM Asquith promised women the vote but backed out of it. This annoys the Suffragettes and Suffragists even more.

1911

Emily Davison set fire to three post boxes and was sentenced six months in prison.

1913

Emily Davison ran out onto the racetrack at Epsom Derby trying to put a Votes for Women banner on the king’s horse. She failed and killed herself in the process.

1914

Last WSPU meeting before WW1

1918

Women get the vote.
